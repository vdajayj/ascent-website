Overview about conference

About the Conference

India's aspirations for achieving a sustainable and self-reliant future are intrinsically tied to advancements in chemical engineering and nanotechnology. These disciplines are essential for addressing critical challenges in renewable energy, green manufacturing, healthcare, and environmental sustainability. Aligned with national goals such as achieving net-zero emissions by 2070, promoting a circular economy, and enhancing India’s Sustainability Development Index.

This conference invites researchers, scientists, innovators, and startups from academia, research institutions, and industry R&D to engage in thought-provoking discussions, showcase pioneering innovations, and explore cutting-edge research areas. Special emphasis will be placed on empowering young researchers and students to contribute to India’s sustainable future.

ASCENT 2025 aims to unite India’s brightest minds and passionate innovators to explore transformative technologies, advance national sustainability goals, and shape a resilient and sustainable future. Together, let us rise to new heights in chemical engineering and nanotechnology, contributing to India’s journey toward global leadership in sustainable innovation. ASCENT 2025 will provide a platform to foster the next generation of scientific leaders.

We invite researchers, scientists, young innovators, and students to submit abstracts not exceeding 250 words in the prescribed format through the provided Google Form. At least one author or co-author must register to present the paper at the conference. The abstract should align with the conference themes and demonstrate originality, innovation, and relevance.

themetic-areas

ASCENT-2025 welcomes research papers on the following topics but not limited to:

  • Sustainable Process Intensification : Revolutionizing energy and resource efficiency for achieving net-zero industrial processes.
  • AI and Machine Learning in Chemical and Nano Sciences: Driving digitalization and smart solutions in manufacturing, materials design, and process optimization.
  • Nanotechnology for Renewable Energy and Water: Innovations in photovoltaics, energy storage, desalination, and water purification to support India’s energy and water security missions.
  • Green Chemistry and Circular Economy: Developing sustainable materials and waste-to-resource technologies to advance India’s Swachh Bharat and resource-efficiency goals.
  • Biotechnology and Bioeconomy: Leveraging bioprocessing for biofuels, biofertilizers, and sustainable agriculture aligned with India’s Green Hydrogen Mission.
  • Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS): Accelerating CO₂ reduction strategies to meet India’s climate targets.
  • Advanced Materials and Nano-engineering: Designing materials for enhanced energy efficiency, healthcare applications, and sustainable manufacturing.
  • Hydrogen Economy and Energy Transition: Supporting the National Green Hydrogen Mission through innovative production, storage, and utilization strategies.
  • Entrepreneurship and Startups in Sustainability: Fostering a startup ecosystem to drive the 'Make in India' initiatives in sustainable technologies.
  • Skill Development and Capacity Building: Empowering young researchers and students with the skills to lead India’s sustainable technology revolution.

    About the Institute and Warangal

    National Institute of Technology, Warangal (Deemed University), formerly known as Regional Engineering College, was established in 1959. The Institute is constantly striving to achieve higher levels of technical excellence and its NIRF ranking 2021 is 23rd in engineering category. There are 14 Departments offering 8 undergraduate and 31 post-graduate programs besides doctoral programs. About 5500 National and International students stay on the campus. It is a fully residential campus sprawling over 250 acres with excellent infrastructure in the form of state-of-the-art library, seminar halls, guest houses and laboratories. The Institute has shown excellent initiative in bagging big projects of substance and quality in the past few years.

    Warangal is known for its rich historical and cultural heritage. It is situated at a distance of 190 km from Hyderabad airport. Warangal is well connected by rail and road. National Institute of Technology, Warangal campus is 2 km away from Kazipet railway station and 12 km away from Warangal railway station.

    About the Department

    Established in 1954, the Department of Chemical Engineering, NIT Warangal, celebrates its Diamond Jubilee (1954–2024), marking six decades of excellence in education, research, and innovation. The department has emerged as a hub for cutting-edge research, evidenced by numerous patents, technology transfers, and industry collaborations. Faculty and researchers actively contribute to frontier areas, including sustainability, process intensification, nanotechnology, and green energy solutions, driving impactful advancements. The department has a distinguished legacy of developing technologies that have been successfully transferred to industries, fostering the application of academic innovation in real-world contexts. It is also home to prestigious projects supported by government agencies and industries, underscoring its commitment to solving critical societal and industrial challenges.

    Our alumni network spans the globe, with graduates occupying leadership positions in multinational corporations, academia, research institutions, and policy-making bodies. They have significantly influenced chemical and allied industries worldwide, reflecting the transformative education and training imparted at NIT Warangal.

    We have conducted three editions of the International Conference, INCEEE (2016, 2019, 2023), AOSS - 2023, and SCHEMCON-2022. As we commemorate this milestone, the department reaffirms its dedication to fostering innovation, nurturing talent, and shaping the future of chemical engineering through excellence in research, education, and global engagement

    TOURIST ATTRACTIONS

    Warangal encompasses every-thing that perfectly describes Telangana – a historic legacy, revolutionary nerve and spicy food. The Kakatiya dynasty, which was the first to consolidate and rule the entire area of Telangana, had its capital here. The city’s history dates back to about 12th century CE, when the Kakatiyas built the Warangal Fort and subsequently left behind some architectural master-pieces and infrastructure in this region, which have no parallel in the state. The region is also home to several handicrafts, which Telangana is famous for – Pembarthi metalwork and Cheriyal paintings both belong to this district. Today, Warangal is famous for its educational institutions. The city is home to the prestigious Kakatiya University, Mahatma Gandhi Medical College and a National Institute of Technology. It is the second-fastest growing city in Telangana, after Hyderabad, and in 2014, it was included in the Heritage City Dev-elopment and Augmentation Yojana by the Government of India, along with 11 other heritage cities.
